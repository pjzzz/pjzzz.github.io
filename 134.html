The very best of the meat would be roasted straight away for the feast that marked
the Autumn Passage. The celebrations would last for three days of sheer exuberance,
dancing and stories that Old Thrashbarg would tell of how the hunt had gone, stories
that he would have been busy sitting making up in his hut while the rest of the village
was out doing the actual hunting.
And then the very, very best of the meat would be saved from the feast and delivered
cold to the Sandwich Maker. And the Sandwich Maker would exercise on it the skills
that
he had brought to them from the gods, and make the exquisite Sandwiches of the Third
Season, of which the whole village would partake before beginning, the next day, to
prepare themselves for the rigours of the coming winter.
