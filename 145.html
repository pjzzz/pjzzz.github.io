"Put my bag in the front bedroom, Calpurnia," was the first thing Aunt Alexandra
said. "Jean Louise, stop scratching your head," was the second thing she said.
Calpurnia picked up Aunty's heavy suitcase and opened the door. "I'll take it," said
Jem, and took it. I heard the suitcase hit the bedroom floor with a thump. The
sound had a dull permanence about it.
"Have you come for a visit, Aunty?" I asked. Aunt Alexandra's visits from the
Landing were rare, and she traveled in state. She owned a bright green square
Buick and a black chauffeur, both kept in an unhealthy state of tidiness, but today
they were nowhere to be seen.
"Didn't your father tell you?" she asked.
Jem and I shook our heads.
"Probably he forgot. He's not in yet, is he?"
"Nome, he doesn't usually get back till late afternoon," said Jem.
"Well, your father and I decided it was time I came to stay with you for a while."
"For a while" in Maycomb meant anything from three days to thirty years. Jem and
I exchanged glances.
"Jem's growing up now and you are too," she said to me. "We decided that it
would be best for you to have some feminine influence. It won't be many years,
Jean Louise, before you become interested in clothes and boys-"
I could have made several answers to this: Cal's a girl, it would be manyyears
before I would be interested in boys, I would never be interested in clothes... but I
kept quiet.
"What about Uncle Jimmy?" asked Jem. "Is he comin', too?"
