'Stupid! Stupid! Stupid! Stupid!'
Random turned and ran off again. Arthur couldn't keep up
with her and at last he had to sit down for a bit and let the pain in his leg subside.
The turmoil in his head he didn't know what to do with at all.
He hobbled into the village an hour later. It was getting dark. The villagers he
passed said hello, but there was a sense of nervousness and of not quite knowing what
was going on or what to do about it in the air. Old Thrashbarg had been seen pulling on
his beard a fair bit and looking at the moon, and that was not a good sign either.
Arthur went into his hut.
Random was sitting hunched quietly over the table.
'I'm sorry,' she said. 'I'm so sorry.'
'That's all right,' said Arthur as gently as he knew how. 'It's
good to, well, to have a little chat. There's so much we have to learn and understand
about each other, and life isn't, well it isn't all just tea and sandwiches . . .'
'I'm so sorry,' she said again, sobbing.
Arthur went up to her and put his arm round her shoulder.
She didn't resist or pull away. Then Arthur saw what it was she was so sorry about.
