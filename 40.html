It seemed beyond all hope that she should be given her chance again. Had he found
her? Had he come back?
The craft dropped down and down until at last it had settled quietly on her lawn. It
didn't look exactly like the one she had seen departing all those years ago, she
thought, but flashing lights in the night sky are hard to resolve into clear shapes.
Silence.
Then a click and a hum.
Then another click and another hum. Click hum, click hum.
A doorway slid open, spilling light towards her across the
lawn.
She waited, tingling.
A figure stood silhouetted in the light, then another, and
another.
Wide eyes blinked slowly at her. Hands were slowly raised in greeting.
'McMillan?' a voice said at last, a strange, thin voice that managed the syllables
with difficulty. 'Tricia McMillan. Ms Tricia McMillan?'
'Yes,' said Tricia, almost soundlessly.
'We have been monitoring you.'
'M . . . monitoring? Me?'
'Yes.'
They looked at her for a while, their large eyes moving
up and down her very slowly.
