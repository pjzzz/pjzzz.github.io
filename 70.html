Arthur stared at the boghog, not knowing what to do. The boghog looked at him
questioningly. It tried to approach him, making mournful little whimpering noises. It
moved its jaw pain-fully. It made a sudden leap for Arthur's thigh, but its dislocated
jaw was too weak to get a grip and it sank, whining sadly, down to the floor. The fat
man jumped to his feet, grabbed the stick, beat the boghog's brains into a sticky, pulpy
mess on the thin carpet, and then stood there breathing heavily as if daring the animal
to move again, just once.
A single boghog eyeball sat looking reproachfully at Arthur from out of the mashed
ruins of its head.
'What do you think it was trying to say?' asked Arthur in a small voice.
'Ah, nothing much,' said the man. 'Just its way of trying to be friendly. This is
just our way of being friendly back,' he added, gripping the stick.
'When's the next flight out?' asked Arthur.
'Thought you'd only just arrived,' said the man.
'Yes,' said Arthur. 'It was only going to be a brief visit.
