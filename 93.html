Arthur did as she advised. He almost hyperventilated in fact.
When he felt he was
ready, he held his breath and followed her in.
The photocopier was a big old thing on a rickety trolley.
It stood just inside the
dim shadows of the cave. The wheels were stuck obstinately in different directions and
the ground was rough and stony.
'Go ahead and take a breath outside,' said the old woman.
Arthur was going red in
the face trying to help her move the thing.
He nodded in relief. If she wasn't going to be embarrassed about it then neither, he
was determined, would he. He stepped outside and took a few breaths, then came back
in
to do more heaving and pushing. He had to do this quite a few times till at last the

The sun beat down on it. The old woman disappeared back into her cave again and
brought with her some mottled metal panels, which she connected to the machine to
collect the sun's energy.
She squinted up into the sky. The sun was quite bright, but the day was hazy and vague.
'It'll take a while,' she said.
Arthur said he was happy to wait.
